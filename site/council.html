The Council of 21

Sovereign Governance
for a New Protocol

Twenty-one seats. Absolute authority over the evolution of UltraDAG. Not a committee. Not a board. A council — governed by the DAO, accountable to the network.

Membership is not purchased.
It is earned, proposed, and voted in.

Seat Allocation

21 Seats. Six Categories.

Each seat category brings essential expertise. Technical, Business, Legal, Academic, Community, and Foundation — a balanced governing body designed for self-sufficiency.

Council Authority

What the Council Controls

Council members are the sole arbiters of protocol governance. Every parameter change, every policy decision flows through this body.

I
Emission Rewards
10% of every block reward is distributed equally among seated council members. No staking required — your seat is your compensation. Adjustable via governance (0-30%).
II
Equal Voting Power
One seat, one vote. Every council member has equal governance power regardless of stake or wealth. Submit proposals, cast votes. 66% supermajority to pass.
III
Protocol Parameters
Vote on fee structures, staking minimums, reward distributions, quorum thresholds, emission percentages, and voting periods. Shape the economic foundation.
IV
Membership Control
Add or remove council members through CouncilMembership proposals. The council governs its own composition — fully self-sufficient, no founder required.
V
Network Direction
Guide the strategic evolution of UltraDAG. Prioritize development, approve integrations, set the technical roadmap for the machine economy.
VI
Emergency Authority
In critical situations, the Council can fast-track parameter changes to protect network security. Power comes with the weight of responsibility.
Qualification

The Threshold

The Council is not a popularity contest. It is a meritocracy. Each seat category requires domain expertise — not wealth. No UDAG stake is required to serve.

New members are proposed and voted in by existing council members via CouncilMembership proposals. The same process removes members who no longer serve the protocol's interest.

Once the initial council is seated, UltraDAG becomes fully self-governing. No founder, no company, no central authority needed.

Seat Categories
6 Domains of Expertise
Technical (7 seats), Business (4), Legal (3), Academic (3), Community (2), Foundation (2). Each category has fixed capacity enforced at protocol level.
Selection Process
DAO Governance
Existing council members propose new members via CouncilMembership proposals. Requires 10% quorum and 66% supermajority approval. Fully on-chain and transparent.
Compensation
10% Emission Share
Council members earn 10% of every block reward, split equally. No staking required. Your seat is your compensation. The emission percentage is itself governable (0-30%).
Seat Availability
Maximum 21
Fixed at protocol level. When all seats in a category are filled, candidates must wait for a vacancy created by a removal proposal.

The strength of a protocol is measured not by how many govern, but by the quality of those who do.

UltraDAG Design Philosophy

Expertise Over Wealth
No stake requirement. Council seats go to those with domain expertise, not the largest wallets. Crypto developers, legal specialists, academics, and community leaders — selected for what they know, not what they hold.
Self-Governing DAO
The council governs its own membership through on-chain proposals. No founder, no company, no external authority. Once seated, the Council is the sole governing body — fully autonomous and self-sufficient.
Fixed Scarcity
21 seats mirrors Bitcoin's 21 million cap. A hard constraint enforced at protocol level. Governance power remains concentrated among the most qualified, with per-category seat limits preventing any single domain from dominating.
